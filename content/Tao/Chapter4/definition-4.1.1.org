*Definition 4.1.1* (Integers). /An integer is an expression of the form $a—b$, where $a$ and $b$ are natural numbers. Two integers are considered to be equal, $a—b= c—d$,
if and only if $a + d = c + b$. We let $\mathbb{Z}$ denote the set of all integers./
