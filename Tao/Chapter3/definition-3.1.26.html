Definition 3.1.26

Definition 3.1.26 (Difference sets). Given two sets \(A\) and \(B\), we define the set \(A-B\) or \(A\setminus B\) to be the set \(A\) with any elements of \(B\) removed: $$ A\setminus B:=\{x\in A:x\notin B\}; $$ for instance, \(\{1,2,3,4\}\setminus\{2,4,6\}=\{1,3\}\). In many cases \(B\) will be a subset of \(A\), but not necessarily.
